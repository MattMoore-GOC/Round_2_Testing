Permanent resident photos

Use this guide if you are either:

  • applying for a permanent resident card
  • an application for permanent residence in Canada

Your application guide will tell you how many photos you need to submit.

Use a professional photograph

You can print the permanent resident photo specifications for your photographer

Your photos must be taken by a professional photographer in their photography studio. We need professional photos if you’re applying online or using paper forms. You must include information about your photographer with your application. Photos must be original and not altered or edited in any way.

What is an altered photo?

An altered photo means the photo has been edited or changed after it was taken. These changes can be, but are not limited to:

• adjustments to colour, brightness, contrast and sharpness
• cropping your head and shoulders
• adding a white background behind your image, or cutting and pasting your image into a white background
• editing your facial features, like changing the size or shape of the eyes, nose, mouth, ears, etc.
• removing wrinkles, blemishes, birthmarks or changing hair colour
• removing red-eye or glare
• changing the background to make it white or remove shadow

Your application will be rejected if your photos do not meet these specifications.
